How to Succeed

With Jack Dorsey, creator of Twitter

In 4 easy steps

Pick a Memorable Company Name

It pays to have multiple people on your team. Jack Dorsey said the name "Twitter" was not his idea but a collection of many ideas. The term "Twitter" was found in the dictionary and the rest just fell into place afterwards. "Tweet" became the term for your posts and the little blue bird became the logo.

Jack Dorsey

Make Your Project Personal

"Twitter was born out of a desire to see from a personal level." Jack Dorsey first got a job as a programmer after he hacked a website and fixed all of it's holes. He had access to all of its services in the city. He described the city services provided by the company as a vertical standpoint. Twitter was a way to connect people together. "Whatever you can imagine, you can probably do"

Jack Dorsey

It's OK to Not Know What You Want to Do

Jack Dorsey was asked if, when he and his co-founders first started the site, he had a plan on what Twitter was going to be. He said, “We didn't really have any idea. But that's not why we were building it, we were building it because we wanted to; it was fun. It was something we wanted to see.” It's ok to not have a concrete plan at first so mess around with many ideas until you find one that you like a lot.

Jack Dorsey

Don't Stop After One Creation

After Twitter, Jack Dorsey went on to create Square, a credit card transaction based company that made it easier for small businesses to charge their clients using an iPhone. This invention created by Square and Twitter has made Jack Dorsey so succesful today

Jack Dorsey
